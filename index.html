Understanding Liquidation in Crypto Perpetual Futures

What is Liquidation?

Liquidation occurs when a trader's position is forcefully closed by the exchange because their margin balance falls below the required maintenance margin. In leveraged trading, this can happen quickly due to adverse price movements.

How Does Leverage Affect Liquidation Risk?

Higher leverage amplifies both potential profits and losses. With higher leverage, your liquidation price is closer to your entry price, meaning smaller price movements against your position can trigger liquidation.

Key Factors in Liquidation

  • Entry Price: The price at which you opened your position
  • Leverage: The multiplier applied to your position (e.g., 10x, 20x, 50x)
  • Position Type: Long (betting price goes up) or Short (betting price goes down)
  • Maintenance Margin: The minimum margin percentage required to keep position open

Liquidation Price Formulas

For Long Positions: Liquidation Price = Entry Price √ó (1 - 1/Leverage + Maintenance Margin)

For Short Positions: Liquidation Price = Entry Price √ó (1 + 1/Leverage - Maintenance Margin)

Risk Management Tips

  • Never use maximum leverage - higher leverage = higher risk of liquidation
  • Always use stop-loss orders to protect your capital
  • Keep leverage under 10x for better risk management
  • Monitor your positions regularly, especially during high volatility
  • Understand that leverage above 20x is extremely risky
